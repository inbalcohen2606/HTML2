About Goa

Goa is the smallest and wealthiest state in India. Tourists from India and all over the world come each year to enjoy the beautifull beaches of Goa.

Goa beaches are suited for all kinds of tourists. You can find there very noisy and crowded beaches as well as quiet and relaxing ones.

Goa beaches have a variety of restaurants that serve different kind of food - Indian food, western food, fish and see food, cocktails, fruits and deserts.
Goa beaches have also many options for accommodation, from the most luxsured huts on the beach to the most simple guest houses on the street.

The best time to come visit Goa is between October to May.
The busiest time in year is between December to February.

Panaji is the capital city of Goa. It's nice to take a day or two to explore it too.

North Goa

North Goa beaches are mostly busy and crowded. They are more suitable to the young travelers that like to party.

Arambol
Arambol beach is the northen beach of Goa. It is very popular and can be very crowded at the peak season. It has beautifull cliffs and rocks all around, and a fresh water lake at the north end of it. You'll find there excellent restaurants that serve Indian as well as western food, and play live music or host jam sassions.

Vagator
Vagator is a small, quiet and peacefull beach during the day. Around 9PM the trance parties begin, the famous Goa parties. Vagator beach is divided to 3 sections: Big Vagator - the main beach which is characterized by many Indian tourists. Small Vagator - called also Tel-Aviv beach. That's the Israeli young travelers favorite beach. Laura beach - also called Spagety beach. The Italian tourists favorite beach.

Anguna
Anguna beach is also well known by its night life, bars and parties. The pamous Anguna market take place every Wednsday, you can find there beautifull cloth and good food that you wont get anywhere else. Chapura fortress is close by and you can rent a scooter and get there to watch the panoramic view. In general, renting a scooter/motorbike is highly recommended, it's the most convenient way to get from one place to another.

Banulem
Banulem is a quiet and peacefull beach. There are no bars or parties at this beach. It's for those of you that want to just lay back and relax, or go sailing and look for dolphins. You can also enjoy water sports at Banulem. Colba Beach is close by, it is one of the Indian tourists favorive beaches.

South Goa

South Goa beaches are mostly quiet and simple. They are suited for tourists that want to lay back and relax, practice some Yoga or Meditation. South Goa beaches are also recommended for families with children.

Palolem
Palolem beach is a beautifull beach with soft sand and many palm trees. Some would even say it's the most beautifull beach in Goa. Whenever tide is low you can go to the north end of the beach, sit on the rocks and watch the most beautifull sunsets.

The main street that leads to the beach is packed with cloth, jewelries and decorations shops, and restaurants that serve all kinds of food. There's a variety of accommodation options, from a very simple huts to the most luxured hotels.

Patnem
Patnem beach is the smaller and quieter version of Palolem. It's for those who wants peace and quiet but in a 10 minute walk distance from the main south Goa beach,Palolem. It's also very suitable for families with children. The sea in Patnem beach has many planktons in it. You can go in the water when it's night time and watch your hands and legs glow in the dark.

Agonda
Agonda beach is a very natural and quiet beach. At the north end of the beach there's a small river. You can take a boat trip and watch the fishermen working and visit the fish market. At the south end of the beach there's a cliff, you can go up at sunset time and watch the panoramic view.
